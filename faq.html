1. What is the standard cutoff for same-day delivery?

Orders must typically be placed by 1:00 PM in the recipient's local time zone to guarantee delivery by the end of the day.

2. Are flowers delivered in a cardboard box?

Never. We use a local florist network. Arrangements are hand-delivered in vases or professional floral wraps, fully hydrated and ready for display.

3. Can I send flowers to a hospital or ICU?

Yes, though we recommend checking the hospital's policy on lilies or high-fragrance blooms. Always include the building name and room number.

4. What if the recipient is not home?

The driver will look for a safe, shaded spot to leave the flowers or leave a note with a neighbor. You will be notified via our tracking system.

5. Are there hidden service fees?

We believe in Radical Transparency. The price you see includes the artisanal arrangement and professional courier fulfillment.

6. Do you offer "Midnight Express" or after-hours delivery?

Standard deliveries occur between 9:00 AM and 6:00 PM. For special timing, please contact the local fulfillment shop directly after ordering.

7. How do I keep my same-day flowers fresh?

Change the water every 48 hours, trim the stems at a 45-degree angle, and keep the arrangement away from direct heat or ripening fruit.

8. Can I customize a bouquet beyond the website options?

Yes! Select the "Designer's Choice" option and leave specific notes in the "Special Instructions" field during checkout.

9. Which flowers are safest for pet owners?

Roses, Sunflowers, and Snapdragons are pet-friendly. Avoid Lilies if the recipient has cats, as they can be highly toxic.

10. Do you provide a delivery confirmation?

Absolutely. You will receive an automated email or SMS as soon as the florist marks the order as "Delivered" in our logistics portal.

11. Is Sunday delivery available?

Sunday delivery is available in major metro areas. If your location is unavailable for Sunday, the calendar will automatically offer the next available date.

12. Why did my flowers arrive in bud form?

This is actually a sign of premium quality! We ship some flowers (like Lilies) in bud form so they bloom in the recipient's home, lasting much longer.
